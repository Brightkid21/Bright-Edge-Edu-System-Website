Activity-based Pedagogy and Enriched Engagement

90% of a child’s brain development happens before the age of 5. (source: Harvard Centre of Developing Child). Children have a natural desire to learn. Dr. Maria Montessori recognized that children have an intrinsic motivation to learn even and engage in games and activities, children learn to grasp an object, they learn to stand by trying again and again and they master walking. Physical development activities not only make the concepts more tangible but also appeal to children's diverse learning styles and take advantage of their multi-sensory strengths. If children are physically involved in doing activities, they are more apt to be mentally engaged.
